PRODUCT
1. Product name is "Claim Clash" (two words, title case). Not Claims Clash in branding.
2. Positioning: BUILT FOR TWO. Default is two players at one screen (Team A Blue / Team B Red).
3. Solo mode is optional and secondary. Never lead marketing or UI hero copy with solo.
4. Goal is shared understanding and evidence, not debate wins, scores, or "who won."
5. AI is a thinking tool, not an oracle or authority. Never market Claim Clash as truth-deciding AI.
6. Steelman-first culture. Encourage strongest fair version of the other side before critique.
7. Not partisan. Do not add left/right scorekeeping, winner UI, or team "victory" language.

COPY AND PUNCTUATION (HARD)
8. NO EM DASHES. Never use the em dash character (—) in user-facing text.
9. NO EN DASHES. Never use the en dash character (–) in user-facing text.
10. Use instead: period, comma, colon, parentheses, or a normal hyphen for compounds and ranges
    (examples: "pay-as-you-go", "Grades 1-2", "OPTION A: Install", "Green: API reachable").
11. Regular hyphens in compound words are fine. Em/en dashes are not.
12. Applies to: in-app UI, web pages, installer/tester package text, Store listing copy,
    support/privacy pages users see, export labels, emails to testers when they are user-facing.
13. Creator-only internal notes (MARKETING-NOTES, OUTREACH drafts for Zachary) may use em dashes.
    When in doubt, assume the text is user-facing and ban em/en dashes.
14. Before shipping user-facing files, scan for U+2013 and U+2014 and fix all matches.

15. Paid plan: say "recommend" / "strongly recommend". Never "strongly use".

18. Team names: Team A (Blue) and Team B (Red). Keep colors consistent.
19. Tone: clear, adult, non-condescending. Short sentences beat clever punctuation.
